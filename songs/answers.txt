If the file **songs.db** holds the top 100 songs from one listener in 2018,
their audio aura could be described by looking at the overall sound and feel of those tracks.
For example, if most of the songs are upbeat pop or hip-hop with fast tempos and strong rhythms,
their aura might come across as energetic, outgoing, and fun. If the songs are slower, more acoustic,
or in minor keys, it might suggest a calmer, more thoughtful, or emotional vibe.
In short, their audio aura would capture the general mood and personality of the
music they connected with most that year.

Still, using just one year’s top 100 songs isn’t the best way to understand someone’s musical identity.
A person’s taste changes over time, and the songs they listened to most in 2018 might reflect
a specific phase in their life or whatever was popular then. It also doesn’t account for what they
listened to on other platforms or in different settings. A better way to capture their true audio aura
would be to look at several years of listening history, include data from multiple sources,
and pay attention to how their listening habits shift across moods, seasons, or activities.
That would give a more complete and accurate picture of their musical personality.
